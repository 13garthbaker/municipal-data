Did you know?

The more a municipality is able to generate its own income, the more self-sufficient it is. Municipalities should not be too reliant on transfers and grants from other spheres of government. If they have to rely on transfers for over half of their total operating revenue it is a clear sign of financial risk. Municipalities should aim to generate at least 75 percent of their own revenue.
